# 🩺 DHYAN: Therapist Clinical Insights Guide

This guide is designed to help therapists and clinical supervisors effectively utilize the advanced data analytics and AI observations provided by the **DHYAN Pediatric Therapy Platform**.

---

## 📊 1. Understanding the Radar Analytics
The `TherapistConsole` features a Multi-Axis Radar Chart that synthesizes session data into five core developmental domains:

### **Domain Definitions:**
*   **🧠 Cognitive**: Accuracy in matching, sorting, and memory-based activities.
*   **🗣️ Speech/Language**: Articulation accuracy, vocabulary retrieval, and narrative sequencing.
*   **👀 Attention/Social**: Engagement levels, gaze-following accuracy, and social emotion recognition.
*   **👋 Motor (Fine)**: Precision in bubble-popping and drag-and-drop interactions.
*   **⚡ Executive Function**: Latency in response, inhibition (stopping bubbles), and task switching.

> [!TIP]
> **Clinical Utility**: A "lopsided" radar chart often indicates a specific area for intervention. For example, high Cognitive but low Executive Function may suggest a need for longer "Calm Down" phases or slower stimulus presentation.

---

## 🗣️ 2. Interpreting AI Speech Metrics
The AI Speech Sparkles module provides automated feedback based on three critical acoustic and semantic markers:

1.  **Latency (ms)**: The time between the prompt and the child’s vocalization. 
    *   *Interpretation*: Stable or decreasing latency indicates improving word retrieval and processing speed.
2.  **Pause Ratio**: The amount of silence during a narrative (e.g., Story Retell).
    *   *Interpretation*: A high pause ratio may indicate difficulty with sentence formulation or high cognitive load.
3.  **Keywords Found**: Semantic matches against the clinical target.
    *   *Interpretation*: Used to track expressive vocabulary growth longitudinaly.

---

## 📈 3. Longitudinal Trend Analysis
The "Progress View" in the console displays 4-week and 12-week trajectories.

*   **Positive Gradient**: Indicates successful skill acquisition; consider increasing difficulty parameters.
*   **Plateau**: May indicate a need to adjust the **ABA Prompt Hierarchy** (e.g., moving from "Independent" back to "Visual Gestural" to rebuild confidence).
*   **Fluctuation**: Often correlates with external factors (e.g., child’s mood, health, or environmental changes during home-based sessions).

---

## 🎖️ 4. Leveraging the Mission & Reward System
Therapists can utilize the **Mission Engine** to reinforce specific clinical goals outside of direct sessions.

*   **Engagement**: If a child shows low motivation, set a mission for "Earn 5 Golden Stars" to leverage the **3D Sticker Book's** physical aesthetics for positive reinforcement.
*   **Generalization**: Use missions like "Find 10 Animals" to encourage the child to explore semantic categories beyond their preferred topics.

---

## 🛡️ 5. Ethical & Clinical Guardrails
While the **Dhyan AI Companion** provides automated guidance, it is designed to *assist* the therapist, not replace them.

*   **Clinical Override**: Therapists can always manually adjust scores in the console after reviewing the recorded session logs.
*   **Safe Environment**: The "Magical Loading" and "Zen Transitions" are specifically designed to prevent sensory overload in neurodiverse children.
